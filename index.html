Tell your stories to family near and far

Most social media is focused on right now. We post pictures of what we are doing, check in to show people where we are, comment about what everyone else is talking about. People don't usually go into much detail, and what they share is usually seeking the approval of others. Because there is status in having large friends lists, we often don't post freely because we don't know who might be reading.

Family lore encourages you to tell stories to your family, about yourself and about other members of the family. Upload pictures, videos, recipes, not for positive feedback but because you want to share them with the ones you love and who love you.

Your stories will live on long after you are gone.

When there is no one left alive who remembers us, what are we besides birth, death and marriage records? Family Lore gives you the opportunity to tell your stories, as well as to capture the stories that you remember about your relatives while you are still alive to tell them. Sharing these stories enriches those who are here, and preserves this lore for future generations.

FAQ

Who can see my posts?
Every user can belong to one or more families, and only the people in your family can view the content that you create. You determine which families should be linked to anything that you create/upload.
Who determines who is allowed in each family?
Each family selects 3 members to be Moderators. The Moderators can select the appropriate policies for inclusion and fairness. The Moderators approve or deny membership in the family.
What will family members see when they look at my page?
  • Things posted by you
    • Stories
    • Photos
    • Videos
    • Slide Shows
    • Recipes
  • Posts that you were tagged in
  • Comments that you made on your or other people's posts
  • Your place in the family tree
  • Your bio
Can I make local copies of the content?
Yes, you will be able to make local backups of as little or as much of the family content as you wish. Using the search features you can create packages of the content related to critera such as:
  • An individual
  • A branch of the family
  • Geographical area
  • Time period
  • Etc
What happens if someone gets divorced?
The Moderators can disable that person's login, but none of the content related to that person will be removed. Even when someone is no longer in the family, they are still a part of the family history.
